Allow me to re‑Introduce myself

Overview

I graduated from Emory University in 2004, with a BA in Psychology. I always thought psychology was a natural analoge to advertising in how it aims to alter behavior and perception. In 2005 I was hired by PinDotMedia as a Art Director, and stayed with the agency for 7 years. Working primarily in print, it became evident that there was an ever increasing trend towards internet based marketing strategies. As a result of which, I decided it was time to "Sink or Swim" and study Web Development in Chicago at The Starter League. Currently I am a developer at Shafer Condon Carter where I work with a variety clients, on projects ranging the full spectrum of digital needs.

DEVELOPER - SCC

September 2013 - Present

Developer - Schafer Condon Carter - Chicago, IL - www.schafercondoncarter.com

My first assignment at SCC was to rebuild their website, so yeah, I hit the ground running. Its a Rails / Backbone site with active admin CMS, and practically everything is content managed from the full-width background images to Vimeo embeds. My next major assignment was the Nathans Famous website, that was also a Rails site with three static subdomains. Nathan's build was consistent with the core tech stack practiced by the SCC Dev Team, Ruby CMS, Engines for interfacing with 3rd party data Social media, Jekyll for spinning up static Sub domains.

My next major project was the Pork Foodservice website which at the clients request was built in wordpress. This was my first foray into PHP and Wordpress however we were able to achieve an equally robust CMS using the Magic Fields plugin. Skip ahead a few pet projects (gardeneers.org / yallamedi.com ) and we Arrive at Kerrygold, which was a shining example of the SCC dev process: Ruby CMS, polymorphic models, backbone views, solr site search. Essentially we were tasked with taking a wordpress site, transitioning it to Rails and improving on CMS experience so that you could actually do more with less effort.

After Kerrygold I worked on two Front End projects as the lead developer Fusion Academy and Winona Capital. The real challenge with Fusion was content, lots and lots of content, and organizing that content in a way thats best for User Experience. Overall the client was very happy with the Front End that we came up with. On Winona we had to come up with slick custom UI behaviors to bring life to otherwise dry content.
